## What Alfonso actually holds

He does not recall the book's terminology here ("virtuous cycle of price") and said so directly when asked. What he does hold is the **underlying causal mechanism, in both directions, reconstructed through his own reasoning** rather than recitation — which is a meaningfully different and arguably stronger form of understanding than verbatim recall.

**Why raising price works**, in his words: higher price increases the customer's commitment, which makes them more likely to actually use the product successfully, "since they pay more for it and are more invested in it and therefore they themselves want the product to be more successful." This lands close to the source material's actual argument (pricing should "sting a little" because invested customers follow through and get better real results) — he got there by reasoning about incentives, not by recalling the specific claim.

**Why lowering price backfires**, also reconstructed via reasoning rather than recalled: lowering price (1) lowers the perceived value of the product, (2) lowers customer commitment, and (3) attracts lower-quality customers "who are less likely to be successful with the product and actually achieve the desired outcome." This is a near-complete reconstruction of the book's actual chain — the one link missing is that this spiral also destroys the business's own margin, which is what actually removes its ability to reinvest in the product or team.

## What's not yet verified as held

- The wine-tasting study (identical wine rated better purely because it was priced higher) — not tested as recalled this pass, though he separately and correctly connected price to perceived value elsewhere in the session, which is the same underlying point.
- The concrete Gym Launch pricing case study — his own business's specific numbers and results. He attempted to recall a supporting example and mixed it up with a different chapter (the "flying to gyms personally" detail is actually from how Hormozi evolved his delivery/fulfillment model, not from the pricing chapter) — flagged honestly as uncertain rather than stated with false confidence.
