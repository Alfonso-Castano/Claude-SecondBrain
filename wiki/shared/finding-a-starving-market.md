## What Alfonso actually holds

This page is deliberately thin — market selection was the one chapter he self-identified as weaker on going into the interrogation, and that self-assessment held up under testing. What follows is genuinely his, not a compressed version of the fuller page.

**Niching down — solid, not just recognized.** He correctly holds that narrowing a target audience increases how much you can charge for what is otherwise the same underlying offer, and — notably — explained the mechanism in his own words with his own example rather than reciting the book's: a diet plan sold generically vs. one positioned specifically for "a lawyer who's working set hours a week and has a set schedule" feels personalized to that exact person, which is what justifies the higher price. He also holds the book's actual phrase for this: "riches are in the niches."

**General pain-point intuition — present, but general-knowledge-driven rather than specific recall.** He reasons correctly that a market in real, felt pain does much of the selling work for you ("you already do have the job of the sell... they already know about the problem"), but arrived at this from general business instinct, not from recalling the book's specific framework.

## What's not yet verified as held

Tested directly and came up short, honestly reported rather than guessed around:

- The book's specific four-part market-selection framework (pain, purchasing power, ease of targeting, growth) as a named checklist — not recalled unprompted.
- **Purchasing power specifically**: only recognized the logic when cued directly ("you can't sell to a market that can't afford it") — he had not connected this on his own, and it's graded separately from genuine recall for exactly that reason.
- The starving-crowd framing and the Lloyd/newspaper cautionary story were not tested as recalled — not enough signal either way, worth checking next time.
- The market > offer > persuasion ordering was not tested this pass.
