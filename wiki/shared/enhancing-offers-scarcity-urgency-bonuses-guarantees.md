## What Alfonso actually holds

This was his strongest chapter in the whole interrogation — he underrated himself going in ("I don't remember bonuses all that well") and performed better than his own self-assessment on every lever tested.

**Guarantees.** He correctly distinguishes unconditional from conditional guarantees, and correctly reasons that a well-built *conditional* guarantee can actually convert better than an unconditional one — "you would only do this if you have a really strong product... even though it's subjective and the customer can just claim they're not satisfied, if the product actually works, they're not going to want to refund it." He ties guarantee strength directly to genuine product confidence, which matches the source material's own framing. His account of what gives a guarantee "teeth" — specificity of the conditions — is close to, but not identical to, the book's actual test (a guarantee needs a stated consequence: "if you don't get X, we will Z"); worth knowing that's the precise gap. He also recalled, without specifics, that guarantees can be given deliberately eye-catching or absurd names.

**Scarcity and urgency.** Detailed, concrete recall: service-capacity limits (a provider only having so much time to take on clients), cohort- or deadline-based access windows ("only during specific times, such as once every three months"), and — notably — the perception-vs-reality nuance: that businesses can genuinely manipulate *perceived* scarcity through marketing even when the underlying scarcity isn't as tight, and that this is a real, if ethically gray, lever. He collapses scarcity and urgency into one causal relationship (scarcity produces urgency) rather than the two separate levers (quantity vs. time) the source material treats them as — a real but minor gap given how much of the actual mechanics he retained correctly.

**Bonuses.** Correctly ties a time-limited bonus to increased urgency, and correctly recalls that bonuses get introduced specifically to address a customer's skepticism or objection mid-sale — "whenever there's some sort of skepticism, to build more trust and to just make the offer seem more valuable."

## What's not yet verified as held

- Two entire guarantee categories: **anti-guarantees** ("all sales final," used deliberately as a strength signal) and **performance/rev-share guarantees** (payment contingent on results).
- The precise "stated consequence" test for guarantee teeth (see above — he has an adjacent but different criterion).
- The clean scarcity ≠ urgency distinction (quantity vs. time) as two separate levers rather than one causing the other.
- The tactic of using **other businesses' free products/services as bonuses**, sometimes with a referral commission attached — untested as recalled, and worth flagging as directly relevant to the AI-agency direction he's considering.
